Homework 4 – Law of Large Numbers

Statistics & Cybersecurity | Simulation and Visualization

Definition

The Law of Large Numbers (LLN) states that as the number of independent trials increases, the sample mean (average of the results) approaches the expected value (theoretical mean). In other words, with enough data, random outcomes stabilize around a predictable value.

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \bar{X}_n = \mu $$

Explanation

Suppose we repeat the same experiment many times — for instance, tossing a fair coin. Each toss can be represented by a random variable: \[ X_i = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if Head} \\ 0 & \text{if Tail} \end{cases} \] The sample mean after \( n \) tosses is:

$$ \bar{X}_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i $$

As \( n \) increases, \( \bar{X}_n \) gets closer and closer to the true probability \( p = 0.5 \). This principle is the foundation of statistical reliability and cyber risk analysis, where repeated observations reveal the true distribution of uncertain events.

Formula for Relative Frequency

$$ f_n = \frac{k}{n} $$

where:

  • \( f_n \) = relative frequency of Heads after \( n \) trials
  • \( k \) = number of Heads observed
  • \( n \) = number of total trials
