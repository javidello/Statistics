Homework 4 — Law of Large Numbers

Definition, explanation, formulas, and simulation of relative-frequency trajectories.

Definition & Background

Law of Large Numbers (LLN): as the number of independent, identically distributed trials increases, the sample mean converges to the expected value.

Weak Law

\( \overline{X}_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i , \quad \lim_{n\to\infty} P(|\overline{X}_n - \mu| > \varepsilon) = 0 \)

Strong Law

\( P\!\left(\lim_{n\to\infty} \overline{X}_n = \mu\right) = 1 \)

Bernoulli Example

\( X_i \in \{0,1\},\ E[X_i]=p,\ \overline{X}_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum X_i \xrightarrow{n\to\infty} p \)

Chebyshev Inequality

\( P(|\overline{X}_n - \mu| \ge \varepsilon) \le \frac{\sigma^2}{n\varepsilon^2} \)
